Mission

We believe that we are at the beginning of a multi-decade change in the book industry. Even though digital technology has evolved rapidly, our readers' experience is no different from that of a handwritten parchment. We still turn pages and read cover to cover.

In the early days of computing we used ‘dumb’ terminals. The computer asked us questions and we answered, and then, with a bit of luck, it would do something. The interaction was under the control of the programmer. With Graphical User Interfaces (or GUI) we could click on anything and the computer had to respond to whatever we did. This made computing user-friendly. This is what makes Smartphones smart. It is a Design Pattern called Inversion of Control. Instead of the programmer controlling the interaction, now the users did.

With books we are still at the ‘dumb’ terminal stage. We need to read them in the sequence laid out by the author, following the story ‘linearly’ from start to finish. Can we imagine a world where the control shifts to the reader? Can we make books ‘smart’? Can we make reading ‘user friendly’?

We believe that in the future, instead of being limited to story ‘lines’, we will weave webs and lay them out to capture the reader’s imagination. Where the reader becomes the protagonist and traverses their own journey through the content. That a person will not read a book in one sitting but keep returning and find new relevance every time they do.

Books need to become more ‘Digital’. We want to be at the forefront of this change.

Rig Veda: A Digital Version of H. H. Wilson’s Translation

The Vedic heritage, and the Rig Veda in particular, is the foundation of Hinduism. Yoga, Dharma, Spirituality and so much of what defines Indian thought and culture today, derive from it. It was composed by famous Rishis who are household names, and yet, for many of us, its contents still remain a mystery. This book hopes to make the Rig Veda a little more accessible.

This is a Digital version of Wilson’s complete translation of the Rig Veda to English. It converts all the six volumes into a single, easy to carry ebook. It then embeds a state of the art search capability using hyperlinks and indexes. This enables relevant discovery through Serendipity, where we find what we need even when we set out looking for something else. These, and other pioneering approaches, allow the reader to efficiently answer the question - “What is in the Rig Veda and where do I find it?"
